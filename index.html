Protect Life on Land

Sustainable Development Goal 15 aims to protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and halt and reverse land degradation and halt biodiversity loss.

About SDG 15

SDG 15 focuses on protecting terrestrial ecosystems and biodiversity, which are essential for human well-being and the health of our planet.

Why Terrestrial Ecosystems Matter

Forests cover 30% of the Earth's surface and are home to over 80% of all terrestrial species of animals, plants and insects. However, 13 million hectares of forests are being lost every year while the persistent degradation of drylands has led to the desertification of 3.6 billion hectares.

Biodiversity and the ecosystem services it underpins can also be the basis for climate change adaptation and disaster risk reduction strategies as they can deliver benefits that will increase the resilience of people to the impacts of climate change.

"The conservation, restoration and sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems is essential for sustainable development."

SDG 15 Targets

The specific targets set to achieve the protection of life on land by 2030.

Forest Conservation

By 2020, ensure the conservation, restoration and sustainable use of terrestrial and inland freshwater ecosystems and their services.

End Deforestation

By 2020, promote the implementation of sustainable management of all types of forests, halt deforestation, restore degraded forests.

Combat Desertification

By 2030, combat desertification, restore degraded land and soil, including land affected by desertification, drought and floods.

Protect Biodiversity

Take urgent and significant action to reduce the degradation of natural habitats, halt the loss of biodiversity.

End Poaching

Take urgent action to end poaching and trafficking of protected species of flora and fauna and address both demand and supply.

Invasive Species

By 2020, introduce measures to prevent the introduction and significantly reduce the impact of invasive alien species.

Why It Matters

Protecting life on land is crucial for maintaining the balance of our planet's ecosystems and ensuring a sustainable future.

Ecosystem Services

Healthy terrestrial ecosystems provide essential services including clean air and water, food, medicines, and climate regulation. Forests alone absorb about 2 billion tons of CO2 annually.

  • Forests regulate local and global climate
  • Wetlands filter pollutants from water
  • Soil organisms maintain soil fertility
  • Pollinators ensure food production

Biodiversity Crisis

The current rate of biodiversity loss is unprecedented in human history. Species are disappearing at 100 to 1,000 times the natural background rate.

"The loss of biodiversity threatens all life on Earth, including humans. We depend on nature for our food, clean air, water, and medicines."

Take Action

Everyone can contribute to protecting life on land. Here's how you can help.

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Minimize waste to reduce pressure on natural resources and prevent habitat destruction from landfills.

Sustainable Diet

Choose sustainably sourced food to reduce deforestation for agriculture and protect biodiversity.

Plant Native Species

Grow native plants in your garden to support local wildlife and prevent invasive species spread.

Support Conservation

Donate to or volunteer with organizations working to protect ecosystems and endangered species.
